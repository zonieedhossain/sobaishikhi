# 01 — The Story: Who We Serve & Why

The human side of SobaiShikhi. Before architecture and revenue, this is **who** has the problem, **what their day looks like**, and **how the platform changes it**. Use these stories in pitches, onboarding, and to keep the team anchored to real people.

> Names are illustrative composites of real Bangladeshi situations.

---

## The big picture
Across Bangladesh, knowledge and capability exist in abundance — but they're **trapped**. A skilled technician can't reach learners. A graduate can't find affordable, practical, Bangla courses. A homemaker with a broken fridge has nowhere trustworthy to turn. SobaiShikhi exists to **connect trapped knowledge with the people who need it** — so anyone can teach, and everyone can learn.

---

## Story 1 — Rahim, the learner who can't afford the gatekeepers
**Who:** Rahim, 22, from Bogura. Finished college, no job, decent smartphone, limited data budget.
**The problem:** He wants to earn through freelancing but every good course is in English or costs more than his family earns in a month. YouTube is scattered and shallow; he never finishes anything.
**The pain:** *"I know I can learn if someone just teaches me properly, in my language, step by step — and I can afford it."*
**With SobaiShikhi:** He finds a Bangla web-development course at ৳1,499 with reviews from students like him, watches free previews first, learns on mobile during load-shedding (downloaded lessons), and earns a certificate. Six months later he lands his first Upwork order.
**What changed:** affordable + Bangla + practical + trusted = a path that actually completes.

---

## Story 2 — Shahida, the expert whose knowledge is trapped
**Who:** Shahida, 38, a brilliant home cook in Sylhet who runs a tiny cloud kitchen.
**The problem:** Everyone says "you should teach this" — but she can't shoot or edit video, doesn't know how to build a course, and has no audience.
**The pain:** *"I have the skill. I just don't have the studio, the editing, or the platform."*
**With SobaiShikhi:** She picks **"We Produce For You"** — our team films and edits; she just cooks and explains. Her course goes live after admin review. She earns every month and is recognized as a verified instructor. Later she pitches a niche course idea and we **buy it outright**.
**What changed:** the three creation models (self / managed / buy-out) turn trapped expertise into income.

---

## Story 3 — Karim, the broken-phone problem nobody owns
**Who:** Karim, 30, a delivery rider in Dhaka. His phone is his livelihood.
**The problem:** His phone overheats and drains battery. The local shop quotes a vague high price; Facebook groups give conflicting advice; he's afraid of getting cheated.
**The pain:** *"I don't know if it's a ৳200 fix or a ৳5,000 scam — and I can't afford to be wrong."*
**With SobaiShikhi:** He searches the problem; the AI assistant shows likely causes, a **step-by-step repair guide** with realistic local cost, the exact part to buy, and — if he'd rather not DIY — a **verified technician nearby** with reviews and escrow protection. He fixes it himself for ৳300.
**What changed:** the disconnected broken-device journey becomes one trusted, transparent path.

---

## Story 4 — Mufti Imran, trust at scale
**Who:** Mufti Imran, an Islamic scholar with deep knowledge and a small local following.
**The problem:** Online religious content is full of unverified, sometimes wrong information; sincere learners can't tell who to trust.
**The pain:** *"People want to learn correctly, but how do they know I'm qualified — and how do I reach them?"*
**With SobaiShikhi:** Verification confirms his credentials; the **verified-scholar badge** and admin-reviewed courses give learners confidence. Thousands enroll in his Tajweed course across the country.
**What changed:** verification + the review gate make trust visible, so good teachers rise.

---

## Story 5 — Rakibul, the technician who wants a second income
**Who:** Rakibul, 34, runs a mobile-repair shop in Rajshahi.
**The problem:** His shop income is capped by his town's size. His 12 years of know-how earn nothing beyond the counter.
**The pain:** *"My skill could help people all over the country, but it stops at my shop door."*
**With SobaiShikhi:** He writes repair guides (admin-reviewed), takes paid **video consultations** for tricky cases nationwide, and lists as a **verified technician** for local jobs. New income streams from the same expertise.
**What changed:** one skill, many ways to earn — guides, consultations, services.

## The emotional core
Every story shares one feeling: **"I'm stuck, and the system isn't built for me."** Rahim can't afford the gate. Shahida can't cross the production barrier. Karim can't tell help from a scam. SobaiShikhi's job is to remove that stuck feeling — with affordability, Bangla, production help, and visible trust.

> When prioritizing features, ask: *which stuck person does this unstick, and how much?* If a feature doesn't clearly help one of these people, it waits.
