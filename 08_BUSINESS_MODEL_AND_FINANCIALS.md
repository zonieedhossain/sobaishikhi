## A. Revenue streams (diversified, one audience)

| Stream                        | Mechanism                                     | Take rate                    | Phase |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ----- |
| Course — self-produced        | Instructor films & edits; sets own price      | ~20% (instructor keeps ~80%) | P1    |
| Course — we produce (managed) | We supply camera + editing; higher commission | ~50–60% platform             | P1+   |

| Tools & parts                 | Commission on store sales                     | ~10%                         | P3    |
| Vendor subscriptions          | Seller plans + featured listings              | flat fee                     | P3    |
| Video consultations           | Commission on expert calls                    | ~15%                         | P4    |
| Service bookings              | Commission on technician jobs                 | ~12%                         | P4    |
| Advertising                   | Sponsored products / featured experts         | CPM/flat                     | P5+   |

> The point: **many monetization surfaces on the same acquired user** — a learner becomes a buyer, a consult client, a service customer. LTV compounds across streams.

### Three ways to create a course (supply-side model)

Instructors choose how much they do themselves — each tier trades effort for take rate:

- **Self-produced:** instructor films with their own camera, edits, and uploads; **keeps ~80%** (platform ~20%) and sets their own course price. Highest earning rate, fastest to publish.
- **We produce (managed):** for credible experts (technicians, scholars, cooks) who **can't record or edit**. Our team scripts/shoots/edits/uploads; **platform takes ~50–60%** to recover production cost, turning to margin past breakeven.
- **Idea buy-out:** instructor pitches only the idea; if we're interested we pay a **fixed one-time amount** and own the content (100% of future revenue). Each deal admin-approved. Best for evergreen, high-demand topics.

**Why it matters:** the managed + buy-out tiers unlock otherwise-stuck supply, raise catalog quality, and build a higher-margin owned-content library — a defensible asset, not just marketplace flow.

## B. Unit economics (illustrative)

**Per course sale**

- Avg. price ৳1,400 × 20% take = **৳280 gross / sale** (minus ~3% payment fees ≈ ৳240 net).

**Per active learner (blended, P1–P2)**

- ~1.6 paid courses in year 1 → ~৳450 commission + small store/consult attach.
- Add a subscriber cohort (P5) for recurring revenue.

**CAC vs LTV target**

- Organic-led CAC kept low via free Repair Hub + creators' audiences.
- Target **LTV:CAC ≥ 3:1** within 12 months; the cross-sell loop (learn → buy → consult → hire) is what pushes LTV up without raising CAC.

**Contribution per order (rough)**
`take rate − payment fees − support/moderation allocation = positive contribution from day 1` (content is creator-supplied, so COGS is low).

## D. Cost structure

**Low variable cost (marketplace model):**

- Content is creator-supplied → minimal production cost.
- Variable: payment fees (~2–3%), video streaming/bandwidth, support.

**Fixed / scaling:**

- Team (eng, design, PM) — see Roadmap.
- Moderation/admin staff — **scales with content volume** (the trust gate's main cost).
- Infra (hosting, video, search, AI assistant inference).
- Marketing (kept lean early; scales in P3+).

---

## E. Key drivers & sensitivities

- **Supply growth** (instructors/experts/vendors) → catalog → demand. The #1 lever.
- **Activation rate** (signup → first paid) → revenue per cohort.
- **Cross-sell attach** (learner → tools/consult/service) → LTV multiplier.
- **Take rate** (admin-tunable) → margin vs. supply competitiveness.
- **Moderation efficiency** → cost to maintain trust at scale (bulk actions, trusted-contributor fast-track).

---

## F. Moat (why this compounds)

1. **Trust infrastructure** — verification + review gate + escrow is hard to replicate and improves with data.
2. **Cross-domain network effects** — guides feed courses feed store feed services; each domain raises the others' value.
3. **Bangla content corpus** — proprietary guides/courses become an SEO + AI-assistant moat.
4. **Local payments + reputation graph** — switching cost for both sides.
